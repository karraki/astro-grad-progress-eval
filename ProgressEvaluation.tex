{\large{}\emph{General discussion guidelines:}}

\parindent=18pt
{\large{}\textit{These meetings should be a discussion between the student and 
faculty on both current student progress and future directions. Students are expected 
to do a self-evaluation prior to the meeting and lead the discussion in a positive 
direction. Faculty are expected to be direct, constructive, and communicative. 
Both are expected to come to a consensus on the future direction of the thesis 
project, including a detailed timeline.}}

\vspace{18pt}
\parindent=0pt
{\large{}\emph{Current progress discussion guidelines:}}

\parindent=18pt
{\large{}\textit{Prior to the meeting students should do a self-assessment of their 
progress in each of the listed areas. Additionally, the discussion should contain 
constructive comments from the faculty on student progress. This should address 
both areas where the student is doing well and areas where the student could improve.}}

\vspace{18pt}
\parindent=0pt
{\large{}\emph{Future directions discussion guidelines:}}

\parindent=18pt
{\large{}\textit{This portion of the meeting is extremely important and should 
be addressed in detail. The student and advisor, and to some extent the committee, 
must agree that this is the plan with which to move forward.}}

{\large{}\textit{Please give special attention to revising the student timeline 
with updated research goals and milestone dates. In the event of a currently unrealistic 
timeline for graduation, please discuss the following options a) change the research 
plan to meet the original defense date or b) change the defense date to match the 
research plan.}}

{\large{}\textit{Do not forget to discuss summer funding and research opportunities.}}

\vspace{18pt}
\parindent=0pt
{\large{}\emph{Action Items:}}

{\large{}1. Student fills out ``Meeting Checklist'' and detailed timeline prior 
to meeting}

{\large{}2. Advisor reads ``Meeting Checklist'' and prepares discussion items for 
meeting}

{\large{}3. During meeting timeline is adjusted and signed off upon by advisor 
and committee, including details and defense date}
